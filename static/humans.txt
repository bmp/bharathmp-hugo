Bharath M. Palavalli designs games and simulations to understand
complex adaptive systems. He is currently working on identifying
and understanding the conflict-prone relationship between the poor
and the city; specifically, to improve policy planning and design.

His research interests are in the areas of Game Design, Game Theory,
Agent-based modelling, Complex Adaptive Systems, Policy Design,
Poverty and Inequality, Graph Theory, Information Retrieval,
Information Communication Technologies and their effects.

He is the Co-Founder of Fields of View, a non-profit research group
in Bangalore. Previously, I have designed agent-based simulation
frameworks, games for safety and learning, and have worked in the
areas of infrastructure policy, disaster management and poverty.

He can be reached by e-mail at mail@bharathpalavalli.com, he tweets at
http://twitter.com/bharathmp and his LinkedIn profile is
https://www.linkedin.com/in/bharathmp/. His resume is available at
http://www.bharathpalavalli.com/cv.pdf.
